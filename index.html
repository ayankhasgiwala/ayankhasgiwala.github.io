Point Reyes National Seashore

  • Location:Bear Valley Road, Point Reyes Station, CA 94956
  • Area:110.98 mi²
  • Visitation of 2018:2,397,722
  • Established:September 13, 1962
From its thunderous ocean breakers crashing against rocky headlands and expansive sand beaches to its open grasslands, brushy hillsides, and forested ridges, Point Reyes offers visitors over 1500 species of plants and animals to discover. Home to several cultures over thousands of years, the Seashore preserves a tapestry of stories and interactions of people. Point Reyes awaits your exploration. -National Park Service

  • Area:128.16 mi²
  • Visitation of 2018:15,223,697

Experience a park so rich it supports 19 distinct ecosystems with over 2,000 plant and animal species. Eugene O’Neill National Historic Site

  • Location:Danville, CA 94526
  • Area:0.24 mi²
  • Visitation of 2005:3,652
  • Established:October 19, 1976
America's only Nobel Prize winning playwright, Eugene O'Neill, chose to live in Northern California at the height of his writing career. Isolated from the world and within the walls of his home, O'Neill wrote his final and most memorable plays; The Iceman Cometh, Long Day's Journey Into Night, and A Moon for the Misbegotten.-National Park Service

John Muir National Historic Site

  • Location:4202 Alhambra Ave, Martinez, CA 94553
  • Area:0.53 mi²
  • Visitation of 2016: 49,376
  • Established:August 31, 1964
John Muir played many roles in his life, all of which helped him succeed in his role as an advocate for Nature. As America’s most famous naturalist and conservationist, Muir fought to protect the wild places he loved, places we can still visit today. Muir’s writings convinced the U.S. government to protect Yosemite, Sequoia, Grand Canyon and Mt. Rainier as national parks.-National Park Service
  • A Visited Place:Mount Wanda Nature Preserve

Pinnacles National Park

  • Location:5000 Highway 146, Paicines, CA 95043
  • Area:41.57 mi²
  • Visitation of 2018:222,152
  • Established:January 10, 2013
Some 23 million years ago multiple volcanoes erupted, flowed, and slid to form what would become Pinnacles National Park. What remains is a unique landscape. Travelers journey through chaparral, oak woodlands, and canyon bottoms. Hikers enter rare talus caves and emerge to towering rock spires teeming with life: prairie and peregrine falcons, golden eagles, and the inspiring California condor.-National Park Service
  • A Visited Place:Bear Gulch Resevoir

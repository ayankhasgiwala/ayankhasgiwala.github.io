National Parks In Bay Area

Point Reyes National Seashore

Location:Point Reyes Station, CA 94956--Area:111 mi²--Visitation of 2018:2,397,722--Established:September 13, 1962--From its thunderous ocean breakers crashing against rocky headlands and expansive sand beaches to its open grasslands, brushy hillsides, and forested ridges, Point Reyes offers visitors over 1500 species of plants and animals to discover. Home to several cultures over thousands of years, the Seashore preserves a tapestry of stories and interactions of people. Point Reyes awaits your exploration. -National Park Service--A Visited Place:Tomales Point Trail

Golden Gate National Recreation Area

Location:San Francisco, CA 94123-0022--Area:128 mi²--Visitation of 2018:15,223,697--Established:October 27, 1972--Experience a park so rich it supports 19 distinct ecosystems with over 2,000 plant and animal species. Go for a hike, enjoy a vista, have a picnic or learn about the centuries of overlapping history from California’s indigenous cultures, Spanish colonialism, the Mexican Republic, US military expansion and the growth of San Francisco. All of this and more awaits you, so get out and find your park.-National Park Service--A Visited Place:Golden Gate Bridge

San Francisco Maritime National Historic Park

Location:San Francisco, CA 94123--Area:0.07 mi²--Visitation of 2014:4,256,888--Established:June 27, 1988--Located in the Fisherman's Wharf neighborhood, San Francisco Maritime National Historical Park offers visitors the sights, sounds, smells and stories of Pacific Coast maritime history. The Park includes a magnificent fleet of historic ships, a Visitor Center, Maritime Museum, Maritime Research Center, and Aquatic Park Historic District.-National Park Service--A Visited Place:Maritime Museum

Eugene O’Neill National Historic Site

Location:Danville, CA 94526--Area:0.24 mi²--Visitation of 2005:3,652--Established:October 19, 1976--America's only Nobel Prize winning playwright, Eugene O'Neill, chose to live in Northern California at the height of his writing career. Isolated from the world and within the walls of his home, O'Neill wrote his final and most memorable plays; The Iceman Cometh, Long Day's Journey Into Night, and A Moon for the Misbegotten.-National Park Service--A Visited Place:Tao House
